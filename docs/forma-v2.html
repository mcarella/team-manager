02 / Naming

Why Forma

Abstract concept — chosen Forma Primary

Latin and Italian for "form", "shape", and "formation". Directly references the core metaphor: team formation in football, Kiviat diagram shapes, and the Tuckman forming stage. Two syllables, pronounceable in any language, no negative connotations.

Invented word — alternate Organa

Derived from ORGANIC agility — the methodology at the core of the platform. Warm, approachable, and domain-specific. Works well for a branded methodology context. Less immediately recognizable outside the ORGANIC community.

Concrete noun — alternate Kiviat

The radar diagram that visualizes team health. Precise and technical — signals expertise to the target audience. Risk: too niche, too tied to a single feature rather than the full platform mission.

Positioning statement

Forma is a team composition platform for engineering managers and agile coaches that turns leadership archetypes, cultural values, and technical skills into balanced, high-performing team formations.

Mark meaning

The pentagon represents the five leadership archetypes — Expert, Coordinator, Peer, Coach, Strategist. Five nodes connected by thin lines echo the Kiviat radar diagram that visualizes team health. The Ember center node is the manager's vantage point. When the team is in balance, the shape is regular.

Do
  • Use on pure black or the defined surface color
  • Use on light backgrounds with the dark-fill version
  • Maintain minimum clear space equal to the mark height on all sides
  • Use the mark alone at small sizes (≤ 24px)
  • Keep the Ember center node — it's a brand identifier
Don't
  • Don't place on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Don't change the letter-spacing of the wordmark
  • Don't recolor the pentagon nodes (white on dark, dark on light)
  • Don't use the wordmark without the mark in app contexts
  • Don't stretch, rotate, or skew the mark
07 / Tone of Voice

How Forma speaks

Precise
not cold

Use exact terms: archetype, CVF quadrant, Kiviat score. But always ground data in human meaning.

Analytical
not academic

Reference the methodology naturally. Managers are practitioners, not researchers — skip citations, surface insights.

Confident
not prescriptive

Tell managers what the data shows. Let them decide what to do with it.

Human
not sentimental

People aren't scores. Frame assessments as tools for growth, not judgment.

Write like this
  • Lead with the outcome: "See your team's shape in 3 minutes."
  • Use numbers: "3 archetypes. 1 missing. Here's the gap."
  • Address managers as peers: "Your Coaching score is strong."
  • Active voice, short sentences. One idea each.
  • Use the domain vocabulary naturally: archetype, Kiviat, CVF, Hackman level.
Avoid this
  • No "empower" / "unlock" / "transform your team"
  • No vague org-speak: "culture change journey", "high-performance alignment"
  • No HR buzzwords: "engagement", "psychological safety" (unless quoting a source)
  • No questions as headlines: "Ready to build your best team?"
  • No passive voice, no corporate filler ("facilitate", "leverage", "synergy")
